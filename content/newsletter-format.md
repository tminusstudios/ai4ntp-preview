# AI4NTP newsletter format (recommended starting point)

A first-pass weekly structure to design against and refine. The reusable prompt that generates and
refines this lives at [`newsletter-format-prompt.md`](./newsletter-format-prompt.md).

**Naming approach:** hybrid. Clear functional sections, plus the signature recurring cards we
already make (AI Decoded, Prompt of the Week). Every banner is evergreen, no dates baked in, so the
card art is reused issue to issue and only the body content changes.

**Card DNA (for every banner):** ink `#0F1113` or paper `#F4F1EA` background with subtle film
grain. Mono `//` eyebrow (JetBrains Mono, UPPERCASE, wide tracking, lime-deep `#A8D000`). Fraunces
headline with at most ONE word highlighted in a lime `#D4FF3A` box (Instrument Serif italic). Small
lime dot + `AI4NTP` wordmark in a corner. One lime highlight per card, no more.

## Weekly skeleton (fixed order)

| # | Section | `//` Eyebrow | Fraunces headline (lime word in **bold**) | What goes under it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Intro | `// THE DOWNLOAD` | "What's worth your **attention** this week." | 3 to 4 line personal note from Justin. Sets up the issue. |
| 2 | Headlines | `// HEADLINES` | "The five that actually **matter**." | 3 to 5 AI headlines in plain English, one line of "why it matters" each. |
| 3 | AI Decoded *(signature)* | `// TRANSLATOR` | "[jargon], in **plain English**." | The jargon-translator card: struck-through term, then the simple meaning. |
| 4 | Last session | `// LAST SESSION` | "In case you **missed** it." | Recap + replay link + one best moment or quote. |
| 5 | Prompt of the Week *(signature)* | `// PROMPT OF THE WEEK` | "**Steal** this prompt." | Copy-paste prompt in the terminal-mockup card + one line on what it does. |
| 6 | Sponsor | `// IN PARTNERSHIP` | "Brought to you by **[Sponsor]**." | Sponsor blurb, clearly labeled as sponsored. |
| 7 | Something fun | `// SOMETHING FUN` | "AI did **what**?" | A meme, a wild use case, a light bit. Also the home for the rotating slot below. |
| 8 | Get in touch | `// GET INVOLVED` | "Want **in**?" (or reuse "Talk AI to me") | Register, book a 1:1, apply as a guest, sponsor an episode. |

## The "AI for ___" idea: use it as one rotating slot, not the whole skeleton

Making every banner an "AI for ___" riff (AI for non-techies / techies / fun / science / sponsor)
is cryptic and hides what each section actually is. Better: keep the functional skeleton above and
add ONE rotating spotlight that changes weekly, themed "AI for non-techies," "AI for techies," "AI
for fun," "AI for science." Slot it at #7 (Something fun) or between Last Session and Sponsor. That
gives the series identity you want without losing scannability.

- Rotating spotlight eyebrow: `// AI FOR [THEME]`
- Example headlines: "AI for **non-techies**: the one habit." / "AI for **techies**: under the hood."

## Subject line and preview text

- **Subject:** lead with the single most useful or curious thing in the issue. No "Issue #12"
  numbering. Plain, specific, a little understated.
- **Preview text:** the second hook, then "plus [the prompt / the fun one]."

## Per-issue production checklist

**Swap every week:** the intro note, the 3 to 5 headlines, the AI Decoded term, the last-session
recap + replay link, the prompt, the sponsor, the fun bit (and the rotating "AI for ___" theme).

**Stays fixed:** every banner eyebrow and headline (evergreen art), the intro card template, the
Get in Touch card, and the subject + preview formula.

## Keeping it fresh over 8 weeks

- Vary the **Something fun** slot widely (meme one week, a jaw-drop use case the next) so it does
  not become formulaic.
- Rotate the **AI for ___** theme so it never repeats two weeks running.
- The intro note carries the personality, keep it genuinely personal and short, not a template fill.
- If a week has no sponsor, drop section 6 entirely rather than running a filler house ad.
