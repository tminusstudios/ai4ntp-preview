Here are the tools marketers are actually using, grouped by the job you are trying to get done.

## Research and answer-engine visibility

**[ChatGPT](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/chatgpt)** is the default starting point: writing, first-draft copy, image generation, and quick reasoning. On Episode 002, Ian Kilpatrick used it to generate the first logo for a brand we built live. Free tier; Plus is about $20 a month.

**[Perplexity](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/perplexity)** is the one to reach for when you need *sourced* research and step-by-step walkthroughs instead of a confident guess. It cites where its answers come from, which matters when you are briefing a campaign. Free tier; Pro unlocks the stronger models.

**[Echo Check](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/echo-check)** is the tool most marketers have not heard of yet, and the one that will matter most in 2026. It scores how visible your brand is *inside* ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini. Buyers now ask an AI assistant before they ask Google, so "do we show up in the answer" is becoming its own discipline (AEO/GEO). Ian used it on Episode 001 to measure exactly that. The free plan covers most use cases.

## Prospecting and cold outreach

**[Apollo.io](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/apollo-io)** is the off-the-shelf lead and contact database for building a prospect list. Alec Saluga used it on Episode 002 for cold-outreach data. Free tier; paid plans add credits.

**[Apify](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/apify)** is the scrappier, more powerful option. In the Q&A, someone asked for a free alternative to a paid scraper, and Alec pointed them here: a free-tier Google Maps scraper that pulls a prospect list by keyword and geography. If you have ever paid for a list you could have built yourself, this is the one to learn.

## Content, newsletters, and owning your audience

**[Beehiiv](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/beehiiv)** is where you put the audience you actually own. Social platforms rent you reach; a newsletter list is yours. On Episode 002, Justin Novak drafted a full newsletter in a brand's voice and shipped it to Beehiiv live, for about 20 cents in AI costs. Free tier to start.

The frame from that session is worth repeating: "build it and they will come" is a fallacy. The middle of the funnel, the nurture, is where most teams lose. A newsletter is how you fix it, and AI is what makes writing one every week realistic.

## Brand and visuals

You do not need a design agency to look credible anymore.

- **[Midjourney](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/midjourney)** for logos, moodboards, and visual direction (about $29 a month on the tier Ian used).
- **[Behance](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/behance)** as the first stop for design inspiration and trend-reading (free).
- **[Pexels](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/pexels)** for rights-clear stock photography (free).
- **[Google Fonts](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/google-fonts)** for a professional type system (free).

On Episode 002, Ian built an entire brand identity, logo, palette, type system, and a hosted brand kit, in about 15 minutes. The whole stack cost roughly **$50 a month.**

## Building the actual site or landing page

**[Lovable](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/lovable)** turns a prompt into a live website. It is the lowest-friction place to start if you have never built before, and Alec's pick for getting a clean landing page fast. Free tier.

**[Claude Code](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/claude-code)** is the step up when you need *real functionality*, not just a pretty page. On Episode 002 it built a working AI workout-plan generator, live, wired to an API. Alec's rule of thumb: **Lovable for a fast site, Claude Code for real functionality.** All four operators on stage use it daily.

For the data layer behind a real app, **[Supabase](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/supabase)** is the open-source backend (database, auth, APIs) with a generous free tier. It powers our own live audience tools.

## Automation and the connective tissue

**[Zapier](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/zapier)** is the glue that connects your apps and your AI into a workflow that runs without you. Alec used it on Episode 001 to automate a transcript-to-AI pipeline. Free tier; paid by task volume.

## Capture, voice, and speed

These are the quiet productivity multipliers that change how fast you work.

- **[Wispr Flow](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/wispr-flow)**: prompt your AI tools by talking instead of typing. Alec uses it daily. Free tier.
- **[Fireshot](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/fireshot)**: a free Chrome extension that captures a full-page screenshot in one click, so you can feed an entire website into an AI prompt as context. A small trick that punches far above its weight.
- **[Loom](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/loom)**: async video for handoffs, sales, and coaching. Free tier.
- **[PLAUD Note](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools/plaud-note)**: a pocket recorder that turns meetings and calls into an AI-ready transcript.
