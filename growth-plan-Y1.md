## The Seven Growth Levers

### Lever 1: Shorts/Clips Omnichannel (Activated in S2)

Every episode produces 4-6 short clips. Vertical format, 30-60 seconds, posted to YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Instagram Reels, LinkedIn, and Twitter/X video.

**Why it works:** One episode = one long-form video + 5 short-form videos. That's 6 pieces of content per episode, 288 pieces per year. Shorts have 10-50x the reach of long-form in the first 24 hours. The clips are also your best top-of-funnel ad creative: they prove the format works before someone commits to a 45-minute replay.

**Execution:**
- Assign one person (Ian or Alec) to clip extraction within 24 hours of each live session
- Use Opus Clip, Vizard, or Descript for automated clip detection with manual review
- Post 1 short per day across platforms, cycling through the week's clips
- Track which clips pop off and reverse-engineer the hooks (question-led, controversy, surprising demo)

**Model impact:** 76K shorts views in S2, scaling to 234K in S5. Clips source 5-15% of registrants once the format is dialed in.

### Lever 2: YouTube/Google SEO Compounding (Activates in S2, compounds through S5)

Every episode is a search result that lives forever. The first 48 videos are the investment. By month 6-8, older videos start ranking for "AI for [specific task]" queries and pull in non-registrant viewers.

**Why it works:** YouTube is the second largest search engine. Non-technical operators search "how to use AI for [task]" and your videos show up. These viewers register for future sessions without you spending a dollar.

**Execution:**
- Title every episode for search intent, not cleverness: "AI for Sales Outreach: Live Demo for Non-Technical Operators" not "Episode 12: The Future Is Now"
- Write 200-word descriptions with keywords
- Add timestamps in descriptions (Google indexes them)
- Create 3-5 playlists organized by topic (sales, marketing, ops, personal productivity)
- After 25 episodes, audit which videos get organic search views and double down on those topics

**Model impact:** SEO multiplier grows from 1.0 to 2.2x over the year. By month 12, 40-50% of registrants come from organic YouTube/Google search instead of paid ads.

### Lever 3: Paid Ads Scaled (Activates in S3)

You're at $100/week. The data shows your CAC is $0.70-1.10 even when scaling. That means you can profitably scale to $500-800/week without breaking unit economics.

**Why it works:** Your audience is B2B operators. They're easy to target on LinkedIn, Meta, and Google. Your webinar format is a proven conversion mechanism. Most B2B companies pay $15-50/lead. You pay under $1. The gap is so wide that scaling paid is almost free money.

**Execution:**
- Q1: Stay at $100/wk, build the data baseline
- Q2: Scale to $300/wk. Test LinkedIn lead gen ads with clip creative as social proof. Test YouTube in-stream ads on related channels (AI explainer channels, B2B SaaS channels).
- Q3: Scale to $500/wk. By now you have 25+ episodes of creative to test. Run retargeting to past registrants who didn't show up: "You missed [topic], here's the replay + next session is [topic]."
- Q4: Scale to $800/wk if CAC stays under $1.50. Use lookalike audiences built from your 5K+ registrant list.

**Model impact:** Scaling paid from $100/wk to $500/wk (S3) takes total regs from 9K to 21K. Scaling to $800/wk (S5) contributes to the 62K total. CAC stays under $1.10 because your webinar funnel converts so well.

### Lever 4: Partnership Ecosystem (Activates in S4)

This is the biggest unlocked lever. You're in a niche with complementary non-competing creators: AI tool companies, B2B newsletter operators, SaaS communities, LinkedIn creators in the AI space.

**Why it works:** Cross-promotion to warm audiences is the cheapest acquisition channel after SEO. A partner with a 10K newsletter list who promotes your next session gives you 200-500 new registrants for zero ad spend. The math compounds because you're trading audiences, not buying them.

**Execution - Three Tiers:**

**Tier 1: Newsletter Swaps (Weekly)**
- Identify 20-30 complementary newsletters (AI tools, B2B ops, startup communities, solopreneur spaces)
- Propose: "We mention your newsletter in ours, you mention ours in yours. One paragraph, once a month."
- Each swap nets 50-200 new regs depending on list size
- 10 active swaps = 500-2,000 extra regs/month

**Tier 2: Guest Co-Hosting (Monthly)**
- Bring on guest hosts who have their own audiences: AI tool founders, well-followed LinkedIn creators, community leaders
- They co-host a session, promote it to their list, and you get their audience in your registrant pool
- They get exposure to your audience in return
- 1 guest host/month with a 5K audience = 300-800 new regs

**Tier 3: Tool Company Partnerships (Quarterly)**
- Formal partnerships with AI tool companies (GoHighLevel already on board)
- They promote your sessions to their user base in exchange for being featured/demoed
- These are the highest-value partnerships: the tool company's users are your exact ICP
- 1 partnership/quarter with a 50K user base = 1,000-3,000 new regs

**Model impact:** Partner-sourced regs: 5K (S4) to 10K (S5). This is the lever that takes you from 21K to 32K to 62K total regs. It's also the hardest to build because it requires relationship work, not just ad spend.

### Lever 5: Referral Loop (Activates in S4)

Built-in viral coefficient. Your registrants refer other registrants.

**Why it works:** Non-technical operators talk to other non-technical operators. "I found this webinar that actually makes AI make sense" is a natural recommendation. You just need to make it trackable and give people a reason to share.

**Execution:**
- Add a referral mechanism to registration: "Share with a friend who needs this" with a unique link
- Reward: both parties get access to the replay vault (past episodes) when the referred person registers
- This costs you nothing (the vault already exists) but creates a reason to share
- Track K-factor: what % of registrants refer someone, and how many people each referrer brings
- Target viral coefficient of 0.1-0.3 (each registrant brings 0.1-0.3 new registrants)

**Model impact:** Referral-sourced regs: 984 (S4) to 4,162 (S5). The viral coefficient compounds because referred registrants also refer, creating a loop.

### Lever 6: LLM/AI Search Visibility (Activates in S3, scales in S5)

Being cited in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and other LLM-powered search.

**Why it works:** When someone asks Perplexity "what are the best AI webinars for beginners" or ChatGPT "how can a non-technical person learn AI tools," you want AI4NTP to be the answer. This requires structured, citable content.

**Execution:**
- Write episode summaries as standalone articles (500-800 words each) on a blog or docs section of ai4ntp.com
- Use clear H1/H2/H3 structure with direct answers to common questions ("What is AI4NTP?", "Who is it for?", "What topics does it cover?")
- Create a well-structured "About" page and topic taxonomy
- Get mentioned in existing authoritative content (guest posts, podcast appearances, being quoted in articles) because LLMs train on text
- Submit to podcast directories with full descriptions (LLMs index these)
- Write a Wikipedia page once notability is established (50+ episodes, press coverage)

**Model impact:** LLM-sourced regs: 519 (S3) to 2,575 (S5). Starts slow but becomes a significant acquisition channel by year 2-3 as AI search adoption grows.

### Lever 7: Omnichannel Distribution Architecture (Foundational, enables all others)

Every piece of content flows to every channel systematically.

**The Pipeline:**
```
Live Zoom Session (Tuesdays)
    |
    v
Same day: YouTube long-form upload (search-optimized title/description)
    |
    +--> Same day: Newsletter send (recap + next week's reg link)
    |
    +--> Within 24h: 4-6 clips extracted and posted to Shorts/TikTok/Reels/LinkedIn/X
    |
    +--> Within 48h: Episode summary article posted to blog (LLM-bait)
    |
    +--> Within 72h: Twitter/X thread summarizing key takeaways
    |
    +--> Weekly: Retargeting ad launched to non-attendees
    |
    v
Next Tuesday: Next live session (promoted across all channels)
```

This pipeline means one episode = 8-10 pieces of distributed content, each optimized for its platform. The pipeline runs every week without exception.
