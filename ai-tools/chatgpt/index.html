What is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is OpenAI's general-purpose AI assistant for writing, research, images, and code. You describe what you need in plain language, in a back-and-forth chat, and it responds with drafts, answers, generated images, or working code. It runs in a browser, desktop app, or mobile app, with no setup beyond creating an account.

There's a free tier, plus a paid Plus plan for higher usage limits and access to newer models. Under the hood it's powered by OpenAI's GPT models, the same family of AI that underlies many other products.

How and why we use ChatGPT at AI4NTP

We keep reaching for ChatGPT because it covers a lot of ground. In Episode 002, Ian Kilpatrick generated the first logo for GotoBuild, the AI workout planner, from a Midjourney reference image. Justin Novak flipped that workflow in Episode 004, having ChatGPT write a Midjourney prompt from an uploaded photo. In Episode 003, ChatGPT 5.5 (GPT-5) was the model brain running Ian's live OpenClaw agent, included with his subscription. And in Episode 005, Ian and Alec used it to ideate the bakery concepts Sunday Crumb and Crumb B2B, and to compare image outputs against Higgsfield.

We keep it in the stack because it shows up in almost every build, whether that's a quick image reference, an agent's model brain, or a thinking partner for naming a new company. If you're new to AI, ChatGPT is the reasonable default: general enough for writing, images, or code, and the fastest way to see what a model can do for your work.

Is ChatGPT right for non-technical people?

ChatGPT is one of the more approachable AI tools out there. You don't install anything or write code: you sign up, open a chat window, and type what you want in plain English, the same way you'd text a very capable assistant. If you've never used AI before, this is a reasonable place to start, since it can draft writing, answer questions, generate images, and even explain code back to you in plain language, all from one app.
