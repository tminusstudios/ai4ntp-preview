## 1. Intro / cold open (while people are joining, 0:00 to ~2:00)

Goal: warm the room, get people typing in chat, set the stakes.

**As people trickle in:**
> "Welcome in, everybody. Grab a coffee, get comfortable. While we wait for a few more folks to land, do me a favor and drop in the chat where you're joining from. I want to see how far this thing reaches today."

(Call out a few by location as they come in. "Austin, what's up. Toronto in the house. We've got someone in London staying up for this, appreciate you.")

**Then frame the hour:**
> "Here's what's about to happen. In the next sixty minutes, a company that does not exist right now is going to exist. A real brand. A real website on a real domain. A full go-to-market launch with a live newsletter and real posts going out on socials. All of it built live, in front of you, with AI doing the heavy lifting. And here's the part that makes this different from every other AI webinar you've sat through: you pick what we build. Not us. You. In about two minutes."

> "I've got two of the best operators I know with me today, and a fourth voice helping me run the room. We'll do quick intros in a second, but first I want to put the steering wheel in your hands."

## 2. Introducing the quiz / vote (~2:00 to 6:30)

Goal: get submissions flowing, make the synthesis feel like a magic trick, lock the brief.

**Open submissions:**
> "Link is going in the chat right now: ai4ntp.com/sessions/002/assess. Open it. There are two boxes. One asks what we should build. One asks what we should call it. The name is optional, the idea is not. And I want you to go weird with this. A dating app for houseplants. Uber but for borrowing dogs. A subscription box for single socks. The funnier and more unhinged, the better. You've got about ninety seconds. Go."

**While submissions stream in (read the funny ones aloud):**
> "Okay these are already incredible. Someone put... (read 2-3 of the funniest). Keep them coming. The wilder the idea, the better the company."

*(If it's slow, seed it: "Give me something for dogs. Give me something only AI could pull off. Give me something that is basically a joke but you'd secretly use it.")*

**Trigger synthesis:**
> "Submissions are in. Now watch this, because this is the part I love. We are taking everything you just wrote, feeding it into Claude, and asking it to spin your chaos into five real, buildable businesses. There is no way we pre-baked any of this, because sixty seconds ago these ideas did not exist. Synthesizing now."

**Reveal the five:**
> "Five candidates, live on your screen. Read them. Pick your favorite. Voting opens in fifteen seconds."

**Open voting:**
> "Vote now. The tally is live, you can watch it move in real time. We lock the winner in sixty seconds, and whatever you pick is what these three operators are building for the next fifty-three minutes. No takebacks."

**Lock it:**
> "Locked. We are building [WINNER]. That is the company. Ian, Alec, you heard them. Let's go build it."

## 2b. Agenda overview (right after the lock, ~6:30 to 7:30)

Goal: give the audience a map of the hour without over-promising clean segments. Frame three parts as the *storyline*, set expectation that it's live and parallel.

> "Here's how the next fifty-three minutes go. We're building this company in three parts, and you're going to watch all of it happen live, not in slides, not in a recording, right now."

> "Part one is brand. That's Ian. Ian has been a designer for over twenty years, building identities for everyone from enterprise-grade companies down to scrappy startups. He's going to take this company from a name to a real visual identity: logo, colors, the whole brand kit. And he's buying the domain live."

> "Part two is the website. That's Alec. The second Ian's brand starts landing, Alec is taking it and shipping an actual live website to a real domain, in real time, in front of you."

> "Part three is go-to-market. That's me. I'm going to take what Ian and Alec build and run a real marketing launch off it. A newsletter, social posts, the whole motion, live."

> "One thing to set up front: this is not three neat fifteen-minute blocks where we take turns. All three of us are on mic at the same time, building in parallel, handing work back and forth as it lands. It's a boiler room. So my job, on top of building, is to narrate it for you so you always know what each of us is doing. And questions: drop them in chat any time. We'll grab them as we go and hit the rest at the end. Let's build."

## 4. Go-to-market talk track (your segment, back half of the show)

Goal: one idea, delivered hard. Newsletter as the pillar, the engine you built, then build it live.

**The frame (set the constraint, earn focus):**
> "Okay, my part. Here's the deal. I've got about fifteen minutes, and I could spend it spraying you with a hundred marketing tactics. I'm not going to do that. I want to be singularly focused on one thing, because it's the single thing I've found to be hyper-effective for real businesses."

**The insight (lead gen is easy, the middle is where companies fail):**
> "Here's what I've learned. Getting leads is easy. There are a million ways to get leads, and honestly most businesses can figure that part out. What almost nobody does well is the middle of the funnel. Nurturing the people who already raised their hand. Your prospects. Your existing clients. Retention. The people who already know you but haven't moved yet."

> "And the deeper question underneath that is: how do you stay the authority in your space? How do you keep being the key opinion leader people actually listen to, and stay relevant across every social channel at once, without it eating your entire week?"

**The answer (newsletter as pillar content):**
> "What I've found works, better than anything else, is having a newsletter as your pillar piece of content. One real piece of thinking, in your voice, that goes out on a schedule. And here's the leverage: from that one newsletter, you can spin out a hundred pieces of content across every social channel you care about. The newsletter is the source. Everything else is downstream of it."

**The reveal (the tool you built):**
> "So I built something for myself. One weekend, no engineering team, I just talked to AI until it existed. It's an internal tool, and the whole point is anyone in this room could build the same thing. What it does is help you write your daily or weekly newsletter, in your actual tone of voice, on brand, fast. And instead of just telling you about it, we're going to build the version for [WINNER] right here, live, off the brand and the site Ian and Alec just made."

**Then go into the live build** (Firecrawl pulls sources, AI scores them, compile the newsletter, ship to Beehiiv, generate the LinkedIn and X posts in brand voice, post live). Narrate as you go. The "first five likes win" hook lands here:
> "Posting to our socials right now. First five people to like or comment on this, the brand-kit PDF lands in your inbox in the next ten minutes."
