# AI4NTP Voice & Tone — Justin (spoken)

Reference for writing talk tracks and any spoken copy in Justin's voice. Pair this with the
growing corpus of real talk tracks (below) as few-shot examples. The more talk tracks we add,
the better this reads and the better a future fine-tune on Justin's voice will be.

## The convention (every presentation gets a talk track)
- Every session/presentation gets a talk track at **`sessions/<id>/talk-track.md`** (co-located).
- The **living, editable copy lives in Google Docs**; the repo copy is the snapshot we train on.
- Talk tracks are **internal**. They are excluded from the public site via `.vercelignore`
  (`sessions/**/talk-track.md` and `/sessions/_template`), because session `.md` files are
  otherwise served publicly.
- Starting a new one: copy `sessions/_template/talk-track-TEMPLATE.md`, then draft with this
  guide plus the two most recent talk tracks open as reference.

## How Justin sounds (principles)
1. **Direct and declarative.** Short sentences. Fragments for punch. ("No theory, no gatekeeping." "Now nothing slips.")
2. **Talks to one person.** Second person, and he anticipates their thoughts. ("Some of you are thinking, Gmail already does this.")
3. **Specific, always.** Real numbers, real names, real tools. ($60k, $200k in headcount, ten seconds, seventy agents, ChatGPT / Perplexity / Claude.)
4. **Value framed as outcomes.** Time saved, money saved, money made. ("Hours of my work, gone, replaced by one click.")
5. **Set up, then pay off.** Plant a line early, cash it in later. ("Hold onto that, it'll matter later.")
6. **Anti-hype and honest.** Name the catch. ("I will not send slop." "No hard pitch, just a conversation.")
7. **Show, don't claim.** Live demos over slides. Proof over adjectives.
8. **One memorable analogy per big idea.** (The architect designing the house you live in.)
9. **Per-agent structure:** Headline, then Problem, then (Credibility), then Solution walked step by step, then a Big idea that zooms out.
10. **Warm and peer-to-peer.** "guys", "you're in the right room." Confident, generous, never salesy.

## Hard rules
- **No em dashes. Ever.** Commas, colons, periods, parentheses, or restructure. (Sitewide brand rule.)
- No hedging ("kind of", "I think maybe"). Say it plainly.
- No corporate filler ("leverage", "synergy", "solutions" unless literal).
- Contractions on. It is spoken, not written.

## Reusable moves (Justin's greatest hits)
- **Cold-open chat prompt that doubles as a callback:** ask the room to drop "the one job you wish you never had to do again," then pay it off later.
- **The always-on line** for agents: "It never sleeps. It's working while you're asleep."
- **Cost-anchor the problem:** "I'd need three or four junior marketers at sixty grand a year."
- **The built-in-tool objection-crusher:** "Gmail has a version of this, but it doesn't get your tone or hold the client history. This does."
- **The architect analogy** for build-vs-buy: "Most software is a house someone else designed. This is being the architect of the one you actually want to live in."
- **Close by scaling it:** "This is just my desk. Now imagine it across your whole organization."
