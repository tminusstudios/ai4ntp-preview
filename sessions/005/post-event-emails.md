**WHAT WE BUILT**
### Same brief. Three completely different companies.

You picked "a bakery business." The builders split by instinct and ran:

- **Justin built Milk and Cookies**, a marketplace that connects local farmers, ghost-kitchen bakers, delivery drivers, and customers. By the final demo you could build a weekly cookie box as a customer, upload inventory as a baker, and accept a delivery as a driver.
- **Alec built Crumb B2B**, cookies that close deals. Not a consumer bakery, a sales and retention tool: send logo-printed cookies to a prospect or client, preview the box in real time, place the order.
- **Ian built Sunday Crumb**, a Nashville bakery truck where a customer scans a QR code, invents their own pastry, picks fillings and toppings, names it, and sends it to the truck.

The room's vanity vote leaned toward Crumb B2B. The real winner was the point all three proved: a non-technical operator can now turn a rough idea into something testable in under an hour.

## Send notes

**List:** entire list, Beehiiv, all sessions, attendees + no-shows, no segmentation. Every registrant should already be in Beehiiv via the sync (Source tags like "Live Session 005"). A single broadcast to the whole audience has no rate-limit risk. If you ever route this through the Apps Script mailer instead, keep `RATE_LIMIT_MS = 10000` (10s), never lower it (Episode 001 lesson: a fast default triggered a 24-hour Google outbound block).

**Do these BEFORE you hit send (they are what makes the comment mechanic actually work):**

1. **Flip the YouTube video to Public.** Distribution is the whole goal here, and comments on a public video are what the algorithm rewards. (An unlisted video can still take comments and they carry over when you go public, but public-at-send is stronger for this campaign. This overrides the default "stay unlisted 24 to 48h" note in `youtube.md`.)
2. **Seed the thread first.** Post one or two comments from your own account before the email goes out (for example: "I'd actually use Sunday Crumb, that QR pastry builder is dangerous. Which one would you use?"). An empty comment section is the number one reason nobody comments. Pin your seed comment.
3. **Turn on notifications and actually reply this week.** The offer is "I reply to every comment," and comment plus creator-reply is the single biggest engagement signal you can send YouTube. Every reply you write roughly doubles the comment count on the video. Block 15 minutes a day through Sunday.
4. **The cheat sheet needs to exist before send.** The email promises everyone who comments a "which AI tool for what" cheat sheet (tied to Johan's real request). The [tools directory](https://ai4ntp.com/ai-tools) is the raw material, but the promise is a simple one-page matrix you can drop as a link in your comment replies. If it is not built yet, either build a quick one-pager (Notion or a PDF) or soften that line to "I will point you to our tools directory." Do not promise it if you cannot deliver it. Ask Claude to generate the one-pager from the directory if you want it fast.
5. **Confirm the prize.** The email commits a free 30-minute build session to one random commenter, drawn Sunday July 6, plus the operators' time replying all week. Confirm Ian and Alec are in before send. Announce the winner by replying to and pinning their comment (no email step, keep it frictionless).

**Deadline:** the copy says "by Sunday, July 6." Adjust the date to whatever gives roughly a one-week window from your actual send.

**UTMs:** the recap, directory, and register links are tagged `utm_source=beehiiv&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=session-005-followup`. The YouTube, calendar, and LinkedIn links are intentionally left clean.

**Video ID:** `5r2wSKA6hOA`. It is already embedded on the recap page (`#replay`, `og:video`, `twitter:player`, VideoObject schema), so the show-notes link previews the video.

**Timing:** send while the session is still warm.
