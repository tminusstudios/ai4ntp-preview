Best AI Tools for Marketers in 2026 (Operator-Tested, Used Live)

Every tool here was used live, on stage, by a real operator doing an actual marketing job.

By Justin Novak, with Ian Kilpatrick and Alec Saluga · Updated Jul 2026
The short answer

The best AI tools for marketers in 2026 group by the job to be done: ChatGPT and Perplexity for research, Apollo.io and Apify for prospecting, Beehiiv for owning an audience, Midjourney for brand visuals, Lovable and Claude Code for building, and Zapier for automation. Every tool listed here was demoed live on an AI4NTP session by an operator doing real work.

  • The complete brand stack Ian Kilpatrick used costs about $50 a month: Midjourney at about $29, ChatGPT at $20, and Behance, Pexels, and Google Fonts free.
  • Alec Saluga's build rule: Lovable is strong for a quick site, Claude Code for back-end functionality.

What makes this list different?

Most "best AI tools" lists are written by people who never opened the tools. They rank whatever pays the highest affiliate commission, padded with screenshots from the vendor's homepage.

Every tool below was used live, on stage, by a real operator on an AI4NTP session, to do an actual marketing job in front of an audience. We name who used it, what they used it for, and what it cost. If a tool is here, someone shipped real work with it while people watched.

Here are the tools marketers are actually using, grouped by the job you are trying to get done.

Research and answer-engine visibility

  • ChatGPT

    The default starting point: writing, first-draft copy, image generation, and quick reasoning. On Episode 002, Ian Kilpatrick used it to generate the first logo for a brand we built live, taking a Midjourney reference in and iterating to a final mark.

    Free tier; Plus is about $20 a month.

  • Perplexity

    The one to reach for when you need sourced research and step-by-step walkthroughs instead of a confident guess. It cites where its answers come from, which matters when you are briefing a campaign. Ian Kilpatrick has used it live on Episodes 002 and 006.

    Free tier; Pro unlocks the stronger models.

  • Echo Check

    The tool most marketers have not heard of yet, and the one that will matter most in 2026. It scores how visible your brand is inside AI assistants. Buyers now ask an AI assistant before they ask Google, so "do we show up in the answer" is becoming its own discipline. Ian Kilpatrick used it on Episode 001 to measure exactly that.

    The free plan covers most use cases.

Prospecting and cold outreach

  • Apollo.io

    The off-the-shelf lead and contact database for building a prospect list. Alec Saluga used it on Episode 002 for cold-outreach data.

    Free tier; paid plans add credits.

  • Apify

    The scrappier, more powerful option. In the Q&A, someone asked for a free alternative to a paid scraper and Alec pointed them here: a free-tier Google Maps scraper that pulls a prospect list by keyword and geography. If you have ever paid for a list you could have built yourself, this is the one to learn.

    Free tier.

Content, newsletters, and owning your audience

  • Beehiiv

    Where you put the audience you actually own. Social platforms rent you reach; a newsletter list is yours. On Episode 002, Justin Novak drafted a full newsletter in a brand's voice and shipped it to Beehiiv live, for about 20 cents in AI costs. The frame from that session is worth repeating: "build it and they will come" is a fallacy, and the nurture is where most teams lose.

    Free tier to start.

Brand and visuals

  • Midjourney

    For logos, moodboards, and visual direction. On Episode 002, Ian Kilpatrick built an entire brand identity, logo, palette, type system, and a hosted brand kit, in about 15 minutes.

    About $29 a month on the tier Ian used; a free account gets you around 10 images.

  • Behance

    The first stop for design inspiration and trend-reading. It is where the best designers post their work, so it is where Ian goes to feel out an industry before generating anything.

    Free.

  • Pexels

    Rights-clear stock photography, used on Episode 002 to set the photo direction for the brand kit.

    Free.

  • Google Fonts

    A professional type system for nothing, usable directly on your site. Ian picked Archivo for the Episode 002 build and had the AI pair it with a secondary font.

    Free.

Building the actual site or landing page

  • Lovable

    Turns a prompt into a live website. It is the lowest-friction place to start if you have never built before, and Alec's pick for getting a clean landing page fast. It also has the API built in, so you do not have to wire one up in a developer console, and a "try to fix" button that feeds errors back to the AI.

    Free tier.

  • Claude Code

    The step up when you need real functionality, not just a pretty page. On Episode 002 it built a working AI workout-plan generator, live, wired to an API. Alec's rule of thumb: Lovable is strong for a quick site, Claude Code for back-end functionality.

    Paid; included with Claude paid plans.

  • Supabase

    The open-source backend (database, auth, APIs) for the data layer behind a real app. Ian Kilpatrick used it on Episode 001, and it powers our own live audience tools.

    Generous free tier.

Automation and the connective tissue

  • Zapier

    The glue that connects your apps and your AI into a workflow that runs without you. Alec Saluga used it on Episode 001 to automate a transcript-to-AI pipeline.

    Free tier; paid by task volume.

Capture, voice, and speed

  • Wispr Flow

    Prompt your AI tools by talking instead of typing. Alec uses it daily, and prompted an entire live website build with it on Episode 002.

    Free tier.

  • Fireshot

    A free Chrome extension that captures a full-page screenshot in one click, so you can feed an entire website into an AI prompt as context. A small trick that punches far above its weight, and the reason Alec's first output looked right.

    Free.

  • Loom

    Async video for handoffs, sales, and coaching. Used by the operators on Episode 001.

    Free tier.

  • PLAUD Note

    A pocket recorder that turns meetings and calls into an AI-ready transcript. Alec Saluga uses it, and showed it on Episodes 001 and 004.

    Paid hardware.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best AI tool for marketers?

There is no single best one, which is why this list groups by job. ChatGPT is the default starting point for writing and first drafts. For sourced research, use Perplexity. For prospecting, Apify or Apollo.io. For owning an audience, Beehiiv. The marketers winning in 2026 are not the ones who found a secret tool, they are the ones who chained ordinary tools into a workflow.

How much do AI marketing tools cost?

Less than most people expect. The complete brand stack Ian Kilpatrick used on Episode 002 was about $50 a month: Midjourney at about $29, ChatGPT at $20, and Behance, Pexels, and Google Fonts free. Most of the prospecting and capture tools here have real free tiers.

Are there free AI tools for marketing?

Yes, and several of the most useful ones here are free. Behance, Pexels, Google Fonts, and Fireshot are free outright. Apify, Apollo.io, Beehiiv, Lovable, Zapier, Wispr Flow, Loom, Perplexity, and ChatGPT all have free tiers that cover real work. Alec used Apify's free Google Maps scraper live to build a prospect list.

How were these tools chosen?

Every tool on this list was demonstrated live by a named operator during an AI4NTP session that we recorded. We do not list tools we have not seen used, and each one links to a page showing the episode, the operator, and what they did with it. No affiliate ranking.

Do I need to be technical to use these?

No. AI4NTP exists for non-technical operators. Alec Saluga is a former B2B salesman with no technical background who self-taught AI. The skill that matters is tool awareness: knowing which tool to reach for at each step.
