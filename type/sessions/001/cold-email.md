## Audience reframe: 200+ employee companies

The second send went to companies with 200+ employees. "Fast growing" doesn't land for this audience because it signals startup energy to someone managing a team inside a larger org. Their pain is the opposite of growth: scale, stakeholders, stagnation risk, and the fear of being left behind while their org moves slower than the technology.

The reframe that won:

**Original opener:** "If you run marketing at a fast growing company while juggling keeping up with AI marketing trends..."

**Revised opener:** "If you run marketing at a 200+ person company and feel like AI is moving faster than your team can keep up..."

Why this works for the larger-company audience:
- Names the size directly so the reader knows the email is targeting them specifically, not the startup crowd
- Captures the actual tension: the org is big enough that adopting new tools is hard, but the person reading feels personally responsible for staying ahead
- "Senior Marketers" in the subject line still works because at a 200+ person company it's a real title and identity, not vague flattery

## Winning conversion patterns (reuse for future sessions)

Pull from these patterns when writing Session 002 onward:

- **"Invite Only" framing for exclusivity.** Frames the email as scarce access, not a broadcast.
- **Senior marketer targeting.** Not founders, not generalists. The subject line and opener both reinforce the identity.
- **Promotion / career advancement angle.** The "get promoted for being the AI fluent one" bullet is doing real work because it's the one bullet about the reader, not about AI or the operators. Most AI event copy sells the tools. This sells the identity shift and career upside.
- **Named operators with specific results.** "Alec 3x revenue in under 30 days" and "Ian's playbook for ranking your company in LLM answers" outperform vague capability claims.
- **Actionable timing framing.** "Monday morning" or "this week" creates urgency and signals practical takeaway.
- **No-code accessibility.** "No technical background required" removes a real objection for this audience.
- **Social proof through track record.** The dorm-room exit to the Take-Two founder, and the $50M+ raise track record, establish credibility without sounding cocky when paired with the softened host bio close.
- **Sign-off: "With Gratitude, Justin."** Warm, personal, and consistent across sends.

## Justin's copy preferences (apply across all future cold emails)

These are hard preferences. They should govern every future send.

- No em dashes
- No insider jargon
- Soften anything that sounds cocky
- Punchy short sentences over long constructions
- Conversational tone, not formal
