# Episode 002 · Tools and Resources Mentioned

Every tool, link, and resource named on the live, with one line on what it is and who used it. Cross-checked against the transcript and the Zoom chat.

## Build stack

- **[Lovable](https://lovable.dev)** — Vibe-coding website builder. Alec's pick for getting a clean site or landing page fast, and the lowest-friction place to start if you have never built before. Free tier. (Alec)
- **[Claude Code](https://www.anthropic.com/claude-code)** — Anthropic's coding agent. Alec's pick for real functionality and custom development (it built the working workout-plan generator). Works in the app, the browser, or VS Code. All four on stage use it daily.
- **OpenClaw** — Ian's self-hosted AI agent, a "digital clone" he runs from Telegram (he has roughly a dozen). Pointed at the brief, it produced the full hosted brand kit. Runs on a GPT-5.5 brain in his setup. (Ian) *(no canonical public URL surfaced on the call)*
- **[Wispr Flow](https://wisprflow.ai)** — Voice dictation Alec uses daily to prompt by talking instead of typing. (Alec)
- **[Fireshot](https://getfireshot.com)** — Free Chrome extension that captures a full-page screenshot in one shot, to feed a whole site into an AI prompt as context. (Alec, tip from Ian)

## Brand and design

- **[Behance](https://www.behance.net)** — Adobe's portfolio network; Ian's first stop for design inspiration and trend-reading. Free. (Ian)
- **[Midjourney](https://www.midjourney.com)** — Text-to-image model for logo and visual moodboarding. ~$29/mo, limited free tier. (Ian)
- **[ChatGPT](https://chatgpt.com)** — Where Ian generated the first GotoBuild logo from a Midjourney reference. ~$20/mo. (Ian)
- **[Pexels](https://www.pexels.com)** — Free, rights-clear stock photos for photo direction. (Ian)
- **[Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com)** — Free type system; Ian reached for Archivo. (Ian)

## Domain

- **[Instant Domain Search](https://instantdomainsearch.com)** — Live domain-availability check. (Ian)
- **[Namecheap](https://www.namecheap.com)** — Where Ian bought gotobuild.pro live for one cent; he prefers it over GoDaddy (~60% of the price). (Ian)

## Go-to-market

- **Pulsar** — Justin's own multi-tenant content engine, built with Claude Code. Sets a brand voice, ingests a brand kit, drafts newsletters, and waterfalls them into blogs and social. Not a public product; email Justin for access. (Justin)
- **[Beehiiv](https://www.beehiiv.com)** — Newsletter platform Justin ships to; you own the audience and distribute on your own cadence. (Justin)
