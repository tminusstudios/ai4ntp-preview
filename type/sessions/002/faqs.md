# Episode 002 · FAQs

Pulled from the live Q&A and the Zoom chat, answered in the AI4NTP voice.

### Was this recorded? Can I rewatch and share it?
Yes. The full replay is on the recap page, and everything (the tools, the brand kit, the live site) is linked and shareable. We do not gatekeep. The only ask: point people back to AI4NTP.

### Lovable or Claude Code, which should I use?
Alec built the GotoBuild site in both, side by side, and the room split on which looked better. Rule of thumb: **Lovable** for a fast site or landing page, and as the lowest-friction place to start. **Claude Code** for real functionality and back-end logic (it built the working workout-plan generator). If you're just starting, start with Lovable.

### Can I do this directly in Claude Code, or do I need another tool?
(Ali, live.) You can use Claude Code in the desktop app, the browser, or inside VS Code. It comes down to preference. Alec works in VS Code with the plugin.

### I'm not a developer. Where do I start?
Alec: lowest-friction start is Lovable. Pick a small use case, build a personal site, or replace something you pay for (build your own Linktree). Two things move you forward: being aware of the tools, and actually using them. Ian: follow your curiosity, and ask Claude or Perplexity to walk you through anything as if you know nothing.

### What does this whole stack cost?
Ian's entire brand stack runs about **$50/month** (Midjourney ~$29, ChatGPT ~$20; Behance, Pexels, Google Fonts free). The domain was one cent. Justin's content generation runs in cents: a newsletter draft was 20 cents, three blogs were 30 cents on Haiku, a social post 5 cents.

### How do I get my site found by AI and ranked in LLMs?
Ian: add an **llms.txt** file at your site root, put proper **FAQ schema** on your pages (AEO), get structured metadata right (GEO), and build **programmatic SEO** pages. Fastest path: copy the AEO/GEO starter and llms.txt out of a brand kit and hand it to Claude or your agent to implement.

### Which AI model should I use?
(Mohammad, live.) Justin: tell the AI what you're building and ask which model fits each layer. He uses **Haiku** for digesting and rewriting articles because it's fast and cheap. Don't overthink it; pick one that's consistent and not too expensive, and revisit occasionally.

### Can I get leads for free instead of paying for a scraper?
(Live Q&A on selling AI receptionists to roofers.) Alec: use **Apify's** free tier with the Google Maps scraper, or ask **Claude Code** to build a scraper for your niche. Apollo.io is another option. Then make the dials, and if they don't answer, that's a hot prospect.

### Can I use these on my existing site (Squarespace, WordPress)?
Yes. Give the tool your existing content and brand as context. Alec's broader take: traditional drag-and-drop builders are becoming obsolete; you can rebuild faster and get better site speed with Lovable or Claude Code.
