**If an image is ever needed again, it must be an inline `data:` URI**, never `<img src="/some/file">`: the doc is served from the auth-gated function, so any external asset would be publicly readable and defeat the gate. Recipe that worked: crop to the exact panel bounds by probing pixel colours, resize to 2x the display width, then `PIL` **quantize to 128 colours + optimize** (600x776 -> 48KB raw / 64KB base64, vs 190KB base64 for full-colour PNG; JPEG was similar size but smears UI text). Locked decisions driving the deck:

- **Equity:** dynamic AI-scored (Slicing Pie style) with three guardrails Justin added — seed the pie with prior work (his ~90% head start is credited as starting slices), weekly AI scoring (build-in-public/competition), and a **freeze** at a milestone (first revenue / raise / 12mo) after which the split locks. - **Roles:** Justin owns the media distribution engine (already his lane). Ian & Alec are asked (via the deck itself) to claim their lane, hours, output, KPI. They have not committed hours yet; both trust Justin; both have asked about equity.
- **Monetization:** "Revenue Test 01" = agents-as-a-service ("Designjoy for agents," $4k-$8k/mo flat, grand-slam guarantee). Framed explicitly as the FIRST test to start cash flow and prove the audience will pay, not just watch. First client target: end of July; 30-day sprint, 90-day max window.
- **Backend thesis (the real prize):** the channel becomes an asset to launch our own products through forever — Ian's app **Echo Check**, plus future apps/SaaS from Alec and Justin.
- **Growth model:** conservative base + labeled upside. ~100 new subs/episode at ~$100 spend (CAC <$1); base 5,200/yr at 1x/wk, stretch ~13,000/yr at 2x/wk + 1-in-5 referrals on ~$10k spend.
