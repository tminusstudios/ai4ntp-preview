**006 OUTCOME ANALYSIS (July 15, 2026) — the "133 registrants" number is NOT comparable to prior sessions.** Zoom report header: 131 registrants (+2 cancelled = the 133 quoted publicly), **18 unique viewers**, 14 max concurrent, 82 min. Naively that is a 13.5% show rate vs a 33-42% historical band (Ep1 42/113, Ep2 86/232, Ep3 65/194, Ep4 18/43, Ep5 46/135). **It is a measurement artifact.** The July 9 `api/zoom-drip.js` backfill bulk-registered the entire historical email list onto webinar 006, so most "registrants" never asked for 006. Registration-time histogram proves it: **81 of 131 registered inside a single 2-hour burst on Jul 9 12h-13h PT**, the rest trickle at 1-4/hour. Cross-referencing the Zoom emails against actual `ai4ntp_signups` records (source `episode-006-register-page`) + Eventbrite-tagged rows: **39 people actually asked for 006 -> 12 showed = 30.8%** (normal), vs **92 bulk-added -> 6 showed = 6.5%**. So the show rate never collapsed; **top-of-funnel demand did** (39 asked vs 135 for Ep5). **Eventbrite (new channel, first run) was the best-performing source: 18 registrants -> 7 attendees = 38.9%**, and it did that *despite* a typo in the listing that said **2:00 PST when the session was 2:00 ET**. Two takeaways that will bite again: (1) **never quote raw Zoom registrant counts as demand while the backfill/auto-register drip is running** (the ledger is email-unique so it is a one-time distortion for 006, but any future bulk add repeats it); (2) six sessions have run at six different day/time slots (Thu 1pm, Tue 1pm, Wed 6pm, Thu 6pm, Tue 1pm, Sun 2pm) on an 8-12 day cadence while calling it "weekly", which is its own demand problem. **Justin's call for the July 15 partner deck: keep the public framing simple (133 -> 18), name the new marketing test and the Eventbrite timezone typo as causes, do not do a public walk-back of the 133** (it is already in the recap video + YouTube description).
